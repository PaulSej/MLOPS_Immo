Paris Property Market Update: Now is a Good Time to Buy!

Paris apartment price have fallen 5.4% on average over the last 12 months. Many factors have driven this decline, the main one being lack of access to financing for French buyers due to rising interest rates and French banks greatly reducing lending. Right now, there’s an incredible window of opportunity to buy property in Paris and we don’t want you to miss it!

What has changed?

The French financial authority (ACPR) have given a strong signal of market confidence by stating that they will return to a quarterly announcement of the maximum legal lending rate allowed for mortgages in France in January 2024. The ACPR was forced to change to a monthly announcement in January 2023 after repeatedly stalling the French lending markets by not increasing the maximum lending rate fast enough.

What does this mean?

We are close to, if not already at, the maximum interest rate increase in Europe that was implemented to stop inflation. As mortgage interest rates stabilize, French banks will begin to open up lending again, allowing French buyers to get back in the game. This means that in 2024 Paris real estate prices should stabilize and slowly start to rise and French real estate transactions should start to increase.
